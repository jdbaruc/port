Concepts

With all our research synthesized it was time to mock a story. And figure out if touchscreen was viable (i need to show more about touch here >>>>>>)

Not the Midas Touch. No Touch at all...

Apple sets the benchmark of touchscreen speeds (120fps or 16ms per touch). Meanwhile our competitor, Pioneer, sports 200ms per touch, which barely took kindly to users. We knew we needed to beat that benchmark. In addition to no latency we needed the touch-capable screens to accommodate animations and never crash. Lest we forget we’re producing products which people use in live settings in front of an audience of hundreds or thousands. Hardware touchscreen manufacturers both couldn’t offer an affordable rate nor prioritize a company like Native Instruments, who were ordering less than a million units. Especially during COVID times. This was not financially viable.

What felt like a loss became a blessing as the tactile experience of the knobs grew on the team and the users during testing and prototyping and created an experience where no aspect of your display was ever obstructed by fingers.

Propsition

The crux of software design and my role. This project yielded feedback through a small alpha + beta group, two rounds of user interviews and, finally, in-app feedback widget powered by Appzi.

3 Tier System

Building a rich parameter visualization allowed us to offer more value to our OEM partners to make them stand out from instrument software utilizing the keyboard for general use. Meanwhile the ultimate tier, with full customization, was saved for our very own in-house products. After all, it's quite a bit of effort to tailor each product to the custom experience.
