Task at Hand

With a catalog of over 200 software products, a decade’s worth of pain points, and an incoming company rebrand, orchestrated by a big external firm, a new and improved Download Portal was imminent.

Approach

I gathered all pre-existing pain points, plus my previous research from the KOMPLETE Browser project, and then the minimal requirements for feature parity with the previous download manager and I started drafting...

Wireframes

With outstanding pain points and stakeholder requirements, it was easy to mock an MVP for the developers to get started on so the Alpha Group and myself could continue to explore features and refine solutions.

Feedback

The crux of software design and my role. This project yielded feedback through a small alpha + beta group, two rounds of user interviews and, finally, in-app feedback widget powered by Appzi.

Alpha + Beta Community

I decided on day one of this one-year project to kick off a beta community. A select group of power users who can help refine and cull the UX and corner cases, allowing me to make choices confidently and free up space to focus on design concepts, prototypes, writing spec and solving issues for entry level users. The Native Access 2 beta group, in particular, was an organized and passionate group. We had a lot of fun building this product together.

I experimented with conducting the group on a private external Slack channel as this idea had worked in the past. With pinned builds and easily searchable discussions. But the lack of the platform’s compartmentalization caught up with us and we migrated to something more traditional within a few weeks. The web team let me hack our product forum platform to host the beta group. Any measure required to stay away from Centercode will always be taken.

User Interviews

Solutions to the major pain points were tested and validated during user interviews. We then refined upon those solutions, circling back to the same users. Watch the full feature here.

In-app Feedback

Being my first time working with a web framework I was quick to suggest embedding an in-app feedback tool. We implemented Appzi. And switched to Typeform upon release.

Challenges

No real project is complete without challenges and as Native Access acts as the client which all Native Instruments' products depend on, this proved to be the most challenging project I worked on.

Legacy

Our download manager rebuild ended with design. But go back, past the back-end, and it started with the migration of our Content Delivery Network. Description and the refactoring of our installer’s tooling. Communication between three development teams was anything but efficient and begged a single source of truth. I sat down with the developers and helped them compose an entity map which all people from product could comprehend and reference.

Design System Conundrum

There was much debate over whether Native Access was part of the web experience or closer to the products. It wouldn’t really matter if it weren’t for the two opposing design systems. Lumen, our product design system, won out given it was more modern, more versatile, more components and better documented. Might have also have to do with me being one of the three builders of the system.

New GDPR Requirements

GDPR and the likes had updated their requirements. We needed to be up front about how we’re using users’ usage data and marketing up front. Across our entire product line. On an already packed quarter scope, our team was asking to come up with and implement this sudden requiement

Company Wide Rebrand

A rebranding was wavering with many unknowns. What a shame it would be to implement an entire design system only to have to uproot it. We stripped our design system down to the components in preparation…

Subscription

One year into development of Native Access a top-down company-wide decision to roll out the subscription model came. We already needed more sprints in a quarter to complete our roadmap and now we had to coordinate with many teams to deliver a 5000 user beta of subscription through Native Access in one quarter.
